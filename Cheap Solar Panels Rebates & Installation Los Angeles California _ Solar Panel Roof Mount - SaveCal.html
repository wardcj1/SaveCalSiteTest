Reduction Before Production

Solar Panels are the fastest-growing source of new energy in the country. They were invented by Bells Labs in 1954 with its creation of the first practical silicon solar cell. Since then, solar panels have undergone changes and modifications, and are now used primarily to save energy costs. Solar Panels are devices that capture and absorb energy of the sun to generate electricity or heat. Photovoltaic modules which solar panels are composed of, use light energy (photons) from the sun to generate electricity through the photovoltaic effect. According to Energy Education, “photovoltaic effect is a process that generates voltage or electric current in a photovoltaic cell when it is exposed to sunlight. It is this effect that makes solar panels useful, as it is how the cells within the panel convert sunlight to electrical energy.”

California and Solar Power

Because of the growing concerns about the environment, carbon emissions, and climate change, solar panels have become more prevalent, accessible and affordable in the country. The state of California, the country’s leader when it comes to “going solar “, encouraged home and business owners to use solar energy thru the California Solar Initiative (CSI) which had a solar rebate program for California consumers that are customers of the investor-owned utilities – Pacific Gas and Electric (PG&E), Southern California Edison (SCE), San Diego Gas & Electric (SDG&E). This program funds solar hot water (solar thermal systems) on homes and businesses, and is called the CSI-Thermal program. This is also available for low-income residents that own their own single-family home and meet a variety of income and housing eligibility criteria, and multifamily affordable housing. Although this program ended recently, Californians are afforded a 30% Federal Tax Credit, providing an opportunity to recoup 30% of equipment, as well as installation costs for an unlimited amount. Other incentives include local rebates, solar renewable energy credits, and a variety of affordable financing programs.
